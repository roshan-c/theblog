thanks to shadow of the erdtree, elden ring is my goty for 3 years in a row

september 24th 2024

in 2022, elden ring released and won game of the year at geoff keighley's game awards. shortly after i picked it up for the first time, and played it all the way through co-op (using the seamless co-op mod). since then i have been infatuated with the world miyazaki and george r. r. martin crafted, and the soulsborne gameplay is something i crave constantly. after that playthrough, i named it as my choice for goty 2023, even as early as january that year! fast forward to june 2024, from software finally released elden ring's only dlc: shadow of the erdtree. before the review formally begins, can i just say i did beat pre-nerf final boss, so yeah, i am that much of a gamer. that being said, the rest of the review will feature spoilers. originally, after defeating mohg, nothing would happen with the cocoon behind him, however, with the release of the dlc, this acts as your entrance to the shadow realm. this place is huge, especically under the consideration that it is optional dlc.
